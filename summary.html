What is this section?

In this section, I wanted to take the time to give a thousand-foot overview of the content, and the general direction of the thoughts. It's similar to the about page, but rather than talking about the idea of this resource, this page will talk about the specific content, and its direction.

The main tools we will be using to start things off are our skills of observations, and pattern recognition (and making inferences.

But before going further, we need to discuss the obvious flaw of pattern recognition and inferences, which is "We don't know for sure."

And that's true. We don't know sure. (If we did, we would not be using pattern recognition and inferences, but rather observations).

That takes us down a different road though, I will address that objection in a different section.

Observations and conclusions from Nature

In this section, we will use those age-old skills of observations, and pattern recognition, and look at things around us, and see if we can recognize patterns

  • Everything around us seems to be an effect from a preceeding cause - what was the first cause?
    • An infinite regress of causes and effects?
    • An effect that caused itself?
    • An Uncaused Cause?
  • Everything around us seems to be following a set of unconflicting laws
    • There are multiple natural laws - but they don't conflict with each other, pointing to a single set of laws
  • Human morality - all humans, regardless of any material circumstances agree that injustice is wrong - right?
    • If we all think that Good is good, and Evil is bad - where does that come from?
  • Objective morality - can injustice ever be right? is there an objective sense of right and wrong?
  • Free will - is it an illusion?
