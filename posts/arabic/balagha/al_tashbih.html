التشبيه (al-tashbīh: simile/comparison)

1. Definition of التشبيه (al-tashbīh)

  • **تشبيه** means *comparison*.
  • It is when you say that one thing is like another thing in a certain quality.
  • In English, this is basically a simile (using “like” or “as”).
  • In Arabic, it is usually expressed with words like:
  • كَـ (ka-) = like, similar to
  • مِثل (mithl) = similar to, like
  • شبه (shibh) = similarity

Example from Qur’an:

وَجَعَلْنَا اللَّيْلَ لِبَاسًا
“We made the night a covering.”
Here, night is being compared to clothing because it covers.

2. Parts of التشبيه

A tashbīh has 4 components (sometimes some are left implied):

  1. المشبَّه (al-mushabbah) → the thing being compared (subject).
  2. المشبَّه به (al-mushabbah bihi) → the thing you compare it to (object).
  3. أداة التشبيه (’adāt al-tashbīh) → the particle of comparison (like ka, mithl, shibh).
  4. وجه الشبه (wajh al-shabah) → the aspect/quality they share (the common feature).

Example: زيد كالأسد (Zayd is like a lion).

  • mushabbah = زيد (Zayd)
  • mushabbah bihi = الأسد (the lion)
  • ’adāt al-tashbīh = كـ (like)
  • wajh al-shabah = الشجاعة (bravery)

3. Types of التشبيه

Depending on what is explicit or implicit:

  1. Tashbīh mufassal (detailed) → all 4 parts are mentioned.
    • Example: زيد كالأسد في الشجاعة (Zayd is like a lion in bravery).
  2. Tashbīh mujmal (brief) → the wajh al-shabah (quality) is not stated.
    • Example: زيد كالأسد (Zayd is like a lion).
  3. Tashbīh balīgh (eloquent/intense) → the ’adāt al-tashbīh (like “ka”) is omitted.
    • Example: زيد أسد (Zayd is a lion).
  4. Tashbīh tamthīl (illustrative) → a whole situation is compared to another.
    • Example from Qur’an: مَثَلُ الَّذِينَ حُمِّلُوا التَّوْرَاةَ ثُمَّ لَمْ يَحْمِلُوهَا كَمَثَلِ الْحِمَارِ
      “The example of those given the Torah but not carrying it is like a donkey carrying books.”

4. Purpose of tashbīh

It’s not just decoration — in Arabic rhetoric, tashbīh is used for:

  • Clarification (making an abstract idea clear).
  • Emphasis (highlighting the strength of a quality).
  • Beautification (making speech more eloquent/poetic).
  • Disparagement (insulting by comparing to something lowly).

So in summary: Chapter 1 of Balāghah is all about tashbīh = comparison/simile, its 4 parts, different styles, and rhetorical effects.
