Reasonable Limits - The limits of Reason

Although I mentioned this idea previously, this pre-requisite may not be completely intuitive. So, allow me to clarify.

(and I can totally understand the hesitation if you hear that a "philosophy of living" requires you to suspend any disbelief, but please, let me clarify.)

By suspension of disbelief, I simply mean that you do not require all ideas to be held to the same criteria for truth or validity.

What does that mean?

That simply means that first, we agree that there are different ways of knowing the truth or the validity of things. And, secondly, we do not judge the truth or validity of one idea with a method not applicable for it.

Let me give a simple example. Let's take a blue car. How do we judge that truth of the statement "the car is blue"?

Well, obviously we have to use our eyes. The way to judge the truth or validity of the statement that "the car is blue" is to use our eyes and our sight. We cannot use our hearing, smelling, taste, or touch to validate "the car is blue".
