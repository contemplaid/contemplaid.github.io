Shamāʾil al-Muḥammadiyyah

Overview

Shamāʾil al-Muḥammadiyyah (commonly called al-Shamāʾil al-Muḥammadiyyah wa al-Khaṣāʾil al-Muṣṭafawiyyah) is one of the most famous works of Imam Abu ʿĪsā Muhammad ibn ʿĪsā al-Tirmidhī (d. 279H / 892 CE).

The book is a collection of hadiths that describe the physical and moral characteristics of the Islamic prophet Muhammad. It is considered one of the most important sources for understanding the personality and character of Muhammad, and it has been widely studied and commented upon by Islamic scholars throughout history.

The book is divided into several chapters, each of which focuses on a different aspect of Muhammad's life and character. Some of the topics covered in the book include his physical appearance, his manners and behavior, his interactions with others, and his spiritual qualities.

Overall, Shamāʾil al-Muḥammadiyyah is an important work that provides valuable insights into the life and character of one of the most influential figures in Islamic history.

Purpose of the Book

Unlike his Jāmiʿ (Sunan al-Tirmidhi), which is a hadith collection focused on law and rulings, the Shamāʾil is devoted to describing the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.

Its aim is to allow Muslims to know the Prophet ﷺ more intimately — his appearance, character, lifestyle, worship, and manners — so that love and reverence for him can deepen.

Structure

The book contains around 400 hadiths, divided into 55 chapters (some editions have slightly different numbering).

Each chapter focuses on a different aspect of the Prophet ﷺ, including:

  • Physical description (height, hair, complexion, clothing, sandals, seal of prophethood).
  • Manners and character (humility, generosity, speech, humor, anger, patience).
  • Daily life (food, drink, sleeping habits, walking, laughing, weeping).
  • Acts of worship (prayer, fasting, recitation of Qur’an, supplications).
  • His final days and passing away.

Methodology

Imam Tirmidhi gathers narrations from the Companions and their followers, often citing multiple chains.

The style is not heavily analytical like in his Jāmiʿ; instead, the purpose is descriptive and devotional. Still, Tirmidhi sometimes comments on the authenticity of reports.

Unique Value

While other hadith collections focus on law (Sunan) or theology (Sahihayn), Shamāʾil focuses on the Prophet’s humanity and beauty. It gives readers a sense of closeness, making the Prophet ﷺ a real, living example rather than just a distant historical figure.

Outline of Shamāʾil al-Muḥammadiyyah

Section 1 – The Prophet’s ﷺ Physical Description

  1. Description of the Messenger’s ﷺ physical appearance
  2. His seal of Prophethood
  3. His hair
  4. His combing and grooming
  5. His white hairs (and how few they were)
  6. Use of kohl (antimony)
  7. His sandals
  8. His shoes
  9. His ring
  10. His sword
  11. His helmet, armor, and weaponry
  12. His turban
  13. His clothing (shirts, cloaks, waist wraps, etc.)
  14. His garments of wool
  15. His red garments
  16. His patterned garments
  17. His way of dressing

Section 2 – The Prophet’s ﷺ Food and Daily Habits

  1. The Messenger’s ﷺ way of eating
  2. The types of foods he liked
  3. The kinds of bread he ate
  4. His condiments and relishes
  5. His drinking habits
  6. The vessels he drank from
  7. His sitting and reclining

Section 3 – The Prophet’s ﷺ Character and Manners

  1. His speech
  2. His laughter
  3. His joking (without falsehood)
  4. His crying and weeping
  5. His humility
  6. His forbearance and pardoning
  7. His bashfulness/modesty
  8. His gait (manner of walking)
  9. His leanings (how he would rest, lean, or recline)

Section 4 – The Prophet’s ﷺ Worship and Lifestyle

  1. The Prophet’s ﷺ prayer
  2. His recitation of Qur’an
  3. His night prayer (Qiyām al-Layl)
  4. His fasting
  5. His supplications and remembrance
  6. His seeking forgiveness
  7. His sacrifices (udhiyah, etc.)

Section 5 – The Prophet’s ﷺ Interactions with People

  1. His character and dealings with people
  2. His jokes and humor with children and family
  3. His riding animals (camels, horses, donkeys, mules)
  4. His journeying (travel etiquette, riding, camping)
  5. His distribution of gifts and charity
  6. His generosity

Section 6 – The Prophet’s ﷺ Final Days and Passing

  1. His age
  2. His death
  3. His bequest (wasīyah)
  4. His burial
  5. What has been narrated regarding seeing him in dreams
  6. His names (Muhammad, Ahmad, al-Mahi, al-Hashir, al-‘Aqib, etc.)

Section 7 – Miscellaneous

  1. The Prophet’s ﷺ miracles
  2. The Prophet’s ﷺ intercession
  3. The love of the Sahabah for him and their reverence
